# RfQ: digital access project

# Introduction

We’re building the Migration Museum’s next digital home. 

## Background

The Migration Museum explores how the movement of people to and from Britain across the ages has shaped who we are – as individuals, as communities, and as a nation. Migration is a pressing contemporary issue and is at the centre of polarised political and online debate. But there’s an underlying story of comings and goings stretching back many centuries. And this story goes to the heart of who we are today. 

Britain has thousands of museums, but none comprehensively focused on this important theme that connects us all. The time is right for a highly relevant, accessible visitor attraction that shines a light on who we are, where we come from and where we are going. We stage engaging exhibitions and dynamic events, alongside a far-reaching education programme for primary, secondary, university and adult learners. We have a growing digital presence and convene a knowledge-sharing Migration Network of museums and galleries across the UK. 

We’ve been given the green light for a permanent home in the City of London, envisioned to open in 2028\. A new development at 65 Crutched Friars is under construction, which will provide a state-of-the-art permanent home for the Migration Museum on the lower floors. The new museum will encompass interactive temporary and permanent exhibition galleries, flexible event and education space, outdoor areas and a cafe/restaurant and shop that will be core parts of the museum’s offer, showcasing the significant impact of migration on food and business. 

We need a new digital home to be ready in 2026 to support our movement-building campaign leading up to the opening in 2028\. This includes storytelling and facilitating value exchanges with our evolving business-as-usual operations. 

## Goals

We want to build a website to facilitate engagement and connections with a comprehensive range of audiences, actualising our mission by reaching people who for the most part will not be on location at one of our physical spaces – and many of whom might only ever engage with us digitally. For online users, we shall introduce the museum as a space for compelling stories, as well as promoting deeper connections. 

### Audiences

The audiences for this project align closely with the audiences of the entire museum. We have segmented our audiences into different consumer and corporate groups, with different needs, expectations and user behaviours.

These groups include older ‘high-arts-engaged’ audiences who are frequent visitors to museums, galleries and cultural venue websites, and for whom this website will likely be their key digital interface with us; as well as younger ‘high-arts-engaged’ audiences whose primary digital interaction with us is likely to be via social media platforms. The website also has to cater to ‘lower-arts-engaged’ audiences who may be less familiar with the conventional layouts and navigational norms of a cultural venue website and for whom recognising and understanding the relevance and authenticity of our storytelling content as well as our programming will be key. The website will also need to cater to ‘corporate’ audiences including funders, educators, community groups and artists, who will be seeking specific information relevant to their areas of work. 

Both in-person and online, co-production is at the heart of our approach and we actively encourage contributions, interactions and feedback from all of our audience groups. So the more interactive we can be, and the more we actively listen, the more aligned our digital experience will be with the physical museum and front of house experience that our visitors interact with daily.

### User needs

We have identified user stories aligned to these audience groups, crossing various pathways and platforms. We’ve validated these user stories with our subject matter experts, noting where and how our long term ambitions differ from our expectations for this website relaunch. A number of these user stories are value exchanges with specific departments of the museum, a number of them are more direct actualisations of our overall communications strategy. All are grounded in compelling storytelling. 

These user stories have helped us to identify high-level functional requirements for donating and sign ups, with varying levels of system integration being appropriate. The majority of user stories impact content production and information architecture. Some user stories relate to contacting relevant teams, for which we anticipate that facilitation via email is sufficient until our organisational needs develop further in the future. 

# Vision

We aim to provide digital experiences that are meaningfully equivalent to and complement the inter-personal experiences offered by the Migration Museum. Our digital function underpins every area of the museum’s work and needs to be responsive to those priorities and value exchanges, especially as they need to progressively adapt. 

This website will effectively be the first major substantiation of a new brand, and therefore needs to embody us authentically. It needs to represent us through a period of change, where we can’t predict in detail how we will change to meet every new challenge. The heart of this design challenge is to portray the museum in ways that may have yet to be fully expressed \- although we can certainly give a good steer. 

We **reveal** the human stories and lived experiences of migration and how we got here. We **remix** to show the hybridity at the heart of British life and culture. And we **reframe** the story of migration from ‘us’ and ‘them’ to ‘all of us’. We are story-led, and understand  that stories are often complex and make us feel a range of emotions. We cut across the grain, finding surprising connections; and are participatory, presenting multiple voices from a range of perspectives. And our approach to co-production means that our website needs to showcase a diverse range of aesthetic possibilities, ensuring that the voices we work with are showcased and represented appropriately. 

## Proposed solution

We need to design, develop and launch a Content Management System based website that can facilitate and optimise the user journeys, value exchanges and storytelling that we’ve identified. While we are in a state of change, we need this system to be flexible and provide us with the building blocks to create the digital journeys to support our future movement-building campaign. It will be administrated by our Digital Product Manager and needs to support multiple editors, who may need to publish independently or with approval. 

Following our new brand, the core design challenge for this product is to create something that looks good on its own but can also accommodate and do justice to the maximally diverse range of artistic content that we host and showcase. Our logo is designed to be dynamic, consisting of different combinations of ‘M’ designs that we will be adding to and re-mixing to create an ever-changing brand. 

We think that most of our content can be accommodated using simple post and page content-types, with affordances for tags and categories. We may benefit from some light visual differentiation based on categories. As well as posts, pages that can display lists of content and a separate homepage template should cover our initial content needs. We are expanding our content production capacity and plan to build more complex content capacities in subsequent development and optimisation phases. 

## Technical scope

Our content and data strategies form the basis of our technical requirements. While we welcome all feedback around technical feasibility in particular, we estimate that a content-driven website system will be best suited to meet our needs. As well as a system to manage content, we require integrations with our donation and email sign up systems in such a way that we can iterate on and optimise our individual giving and connection-building experiences. Other systems integrations can be accomplished using Call To Action (CTA) buttons and links, especially as/when those systems might need to change. Cross-promotion of content and activities in relevant and contextual ways is also important to implement in a way that can be optimised. 

Systems and infrastructure relevant to this project include: 

* Donorfy is our business development CRM and we currently use it to process donations from the website, improving which is a must-have priority for integration and optimisation;   
* Mailchimp is our mass engagement CRM with registration being a key CTA, which is a high priority for integration and optimisation;   
* Shopify facilitates many of our transactions, so is a priority for cross-promotion of content;   
* Eventbrite is used for event registrations, likely to be followed by a box office system that we’ve yet to procure, which can simply be the destination for relevant CTA buttons;   
* Instagram, LinkedIn, BlueSky, Tiktok and YouTube are our current social channels that should be signposted, with the ability to embed content from those platforms into webpages;   
* Cloudflare is a desirable platform for us to introduce for security management and we seek support in making the best use of it;   
* Google Analytics (including Tag Manager, etc) is our preferred mechanism for usage and performance analysis. 

# Methodology

We are in a state of change, and so agility is necessary to meet our evolving needs. We’re front-loading addressing our needs based on certainty and urgency, and roadmapping needs that are less pressing or as yet unclear. While we do not want to specify an exact methodological framework for product development within this project, we encourage prospective partners to demonstrate agility as we anticipate this becoming more relevant in the long term. 

This project falls under the governance of our digital transformation agenda, the working group for which holds fortnightly retrospectives. We also meet fortnightly with a Digital Steering Committee, which includes trustees from our Board. Funding for this work comes from Arts Council England. 

Our base of user stories has been consolidated and validated with our subject matter experts, with each addressing specific audience segments and having a matrix of appropriate content streams and KPIs. In the context of this strategy we have produced (and continue to) exemplar content that can be used as the basis for wireframing in relatively short order. While a lot of content strategy and information architectural work has been done already, we encourage our partners to engage with, critique and potentially develop this work further before building upon those foundations. 

We’re aiming to launch our new website by mid 2026, with scope for discussion. We think that launching this website could either coincide with other launches for a fanfare event, or we could launch it more quietly to test for robustness in advance of any fanfare. 

## Resources

We have: 

* A solid governance model, a dedicated product owner, and a team of passionate subject matter experts,  
* A real vision with a robust framework of user stories, audience analysis and actual content ready to go,  
* A new brand ready to be implemented comprehensively and creatively,   
* A data strategy that is directly downstream from our business strategy,   
* Ambitions for successive digital products and projects that will deepen and broaden public engagement and understanding of migration.
